Research Paper Publication:

Title: Segmentation and Classification Techniques: A Comparative Analysis Based on Performance Properties

Abstract:
This research presents a comprehensive comparative study of image segmentation and classification techniques, emphasizing their performance across key parameters such as accuracy, computational efficiency, and adaptability to diverse datasets. With a focus on real-world applications in fields like healthcare and autonomous systems, the study evaluates traditional methods (SVM, Decision Trees, k-NN) alongside advanced models such as U-Net, 3D U-Net, and CNNs. Findings highlight the superiority of U-Net-based segmentation and CNN-driven classification—particularly when applied to multi-modal data—for high-precision tasks like Alzheimer's disease analysis. This work aims to guide researchers and professionals in selecting optimal methods based on specific application demands and resource constraints.
